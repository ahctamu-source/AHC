AHC Overview

Connect students across disciplines to learn, build, and serve at the intersection of healthcare, environmental health & technology with an emphasis on equity and real-world impact.

Mission Statement

Connect students across disciplines to learn, build, and serve at the intersection of healthcare, environmental health & technology with an emphasis on equity and real-world impact.

Goals

  • Translate evidence into action via projects that benefit campus and local communities.
  • Build career readiness through skills workshops (fundamentals of research, data, QI, ethics).
  • Foster interdisciplinary teamwork on problems no single field can solve.

Structure (Sectors & Sample Activities)

  • Medicine & Clinical Skills: case discussions, vitals/triage simulations, clinical shadowing panels.
  • Environmental and Public Health & Policy: Compostify, park cleanups.
  • Engineering & Tech: prototyping nights (low-cost devices), UX for patient education, app demos, workshops.
  • Data Science & Evaluation: R/Python bootcamps, visualization, program evaluation.
  • Ethics & Law: debates on AI, privacy, consent; guest talks from IRB/ethics faculty.
  • Community Engagement: health fairs, school outreach, partnerships with local nonprofits.

Core Programs (Each Semester)

  • Speaker Series (3–4 events): clinicians, PH officials, innovators.
  • Skills Labs (3–4 sessions): literature appraisal, SEM/QI basics, data viz.
  • Project Sprints (6–8 weeks): small teams tackle scoped problems with a faculty/partner mentor. (TBD)
  • Service Days (2 events): screenings, health education, or supply drives.

Leadership & Membership

  • Executive Board: Co-Presidents, Treasurer, Events, Outreach, Projects.
  • Sector Leads: one per sector to run tracks and project sprints.
  • Open to all majors/years; general membership fees for incentives/food (aim for access).

Faculty Advisor Role (Light Lift)

  • Meet every 1–2 months (30–45 min) with exec team.
  • Advise on speakers, community partners, and project feasibility/ethics.
  • Support access to institutional resources (rooms, referrals, IRB guidance if needed).

Resources Requested

  • Classroom/meeting space 2×/month; projector/whiteboard.
  • Approval to use university name/logo per policy.

Risk, Ethics, and Inclusion

  • Follow all university safety, IRB, and volunteer policies; no clinical contact without authorization.
  • Commit to DEI (diversity, equality, inclusion) in membership, speakers, and project selection.
  • Data privacy training for project members where applicable.

Assessment (KPIs)

  • Attendance (target: 40+ active members, avg. 20/event).
  • Projects completed (1–2/semester) with brief outcome summaries.
  • Community hours and partner feedback.
  • Member career skills survey (pre/post).

Draft Timeline (Fall)

  • Weeks 1–2: Interest meeting; advisor confirmation; sector lead selection.
  • Weeks 3–4: Speaker #1; project pitch night; team formation.
  • Weeks 5–10: Project sprints + skills labs.
  • Week 11: Speaker #2; mid-sprint check-ins.
  • Week 13: Community Service Day.
  • Week 15: Showcase Night (posters, lightning talks).
